#Nutrient budgets
Research into regional-, national-, and global-scale budgets of nitrogen or phosphorus. Topics should cover methods to estimate nitrogen or phosphorus inputs (such as fertilizer application, sewage discharge), outputs (such as removal by crop uptake or processes like denitrification), and recycling (such as the conversion of fertilizer applied to crops that are fed to farm animals and produce manure). Prioritize global research and research in North America and Europe

#Nutrient transport
Research into regional and continental modeling of nitrogen or phosphorus transport by rivers. Topics should include machine learning and approaches that utilize neural networks as well as simpler statistical approaches. Prioritize research in North America and Europe

#Emerging methodologies
Machine Learning and AI Research on the use of machine learning and AI to advance the measurement and monitoring of water quality and restoration or meditation efforts. This includes the development of new foundation models across modalities (such as text, remote sensing, and images) with restoration or mediation applications, as well as novel benchmarks that measure AI capabilities in this field. Additionally, we welcome thought leadership and perspectives on the promises and perils of integrating AI into water quality monitoring and restoration practices.

#Causal Impact
Evaluations of nitrogen or phosphorus loading and their resulting eutrophication outcomes. Submissions should encompass both methodological developments and practical applications, with a particular interest in spatial impact evaluation and the use of causal machine learning (ML).

#Thought leadership
Perspectives on the current state of limnological science, including evaluations of what is and is not working in monitoring and restoration activities and policy. This section welcomes reflections on the directions taken by actors (governments, NGOs, and private entities), analyses of past successes and failures, and strategic visions for the future of the field. Prioritize North America and Europe.
